First major project

A public utility project

  • FRAP
  • UI/UX designer
  • 2018 - 2020

Our objective: facilitate the rapidity of the interventions

Three years ago, Paul, in discussing with some of his colleagues, realised that the enormous registers in paper format, used in intervention, sometimes made them lose precious seconds. It was that digital support could save firefighters valuable time in the event of a fire emergency. interventions. It is from this observation that we set out to create the Fire Rescue Assistant project. Programm (FRAP), a digital decision support solution for firefighters. Accessible on the shelf, our application is the logbook and information library that replaces all paper media that are still in use. It has been designed to optimise the management and the duration of fire brigade interventions.

FRAP : A tablet application

After numerous versions of the prototype, which allowed us to to refine our specifications without having to make us recoding the application each time, we have managed to create a new Functional application supported by content hosted on a server. The result of our project is an application for tablet. This allows users to view all of the information necessary for the intervention of the fire brigade: location fire hydrants and access areas, plan of the boiler room of the target building, follow-up of the intervention with location of the different vehicles and their connection to the hydraulic network and monitoring the spread of the fire. Everything is accessible in only a few clicks. We also offer assistance in the choice of mouths or fire hydrants, depending on the characteristics of the material used and the target building (height, distance...). FRAP aims to help firefighters at the heart of the action. Our application is intended to be used on the site of the fire station. intervention by several users (the head of the apparatus and the head of the column) thanks to the possibility of multiple and simultaneous use.

First prize at the 2018 Innov Seeds Awards

My role

After spending a year developing our application in our corner, I realized that we could not achieve anything concrete without changing our working methods. So I let go of the code and took the initiative to proceed following the user-centred design method. With Paul, we therefore went to intervene with heads of apparatus and columns. Supported by a model made under Figma, we have been able to adjust the features and navigation to best fit the needs of our customers. possible to its future users. In addition to taking charge of the model, I also took care of the visual aspect of the project by giving shape to posters and flyers.
