A first (real) step into working life

  • Trainee in UI/UX Design / Development

In a nutshell

During my internship at DSP (Dassault Syst√®mes Provence), I had the opportunity to confront myself with what are real missions in companies. As part of a team developing an application for CATIA, I was in charge of proposing a new graphical interface for a central tool for application. It had to be more modern, but not too far removed from the spirit of application. And of course, it had to be intuitive. This tool having the ability to display in different ways, I had to study more than 300 different cases for elements the component. A work that might have seemed pharaonic, but with meticulousness and organisation, was entirely feasible.

To bring all these designs to life, I have integrated them into an interactive prototype, allowing for then to simulate the actual use of the tool. In order to do this, I also had to conduct a study detailed description of the various prototyping tools. And after having tested several of them, I finally opted for for Figma.

After completing the mock-up, it was time for implementation within the code. I then trained on GLSL, a graphic card language that allows interaction with shaders and integration of the my designs in the application.

Without having been neglected, the key word of this training course for me was autonomy and proactivity. I was I have thus received a lot of training and I have consolidated my rigour in my work. I have also had to be able to proposal to deal with changes in schedules due to containment.
