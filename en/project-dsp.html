The adventure in big business

A first (real) step into working life

  • DSP
  • Trainee in UI/UX Design / Development
  • april - august 2020

In a nutshell

During my internship at DSP (Dassault Syst√®mes Provence), I had the opportunity to experience what it is like to real missions in companies. As part of a team developing an application for CATIA, I have was in charge of proposing a new graphical interface for a tool central to the application. This one had to be more modern while not straying too far from the spirit of the application. And of course, it had to be intuitive. As this tool can be displayed in different ways, I had to bend over and study more than 300 different cases for elements composing it. A work that can be pharaonic but which, with meticulousness and organisation, is quite feasible.

To bring all these designs to life, I have integrated them into an interactive prototype, allowing for then to simulate the actual use of the tool. In order to do this, I also had to conduct a study detail the different prototyping tools. And after having tested several of them, I have finally opted for Figma.

After completing the mock-up, it was time to implement it in the code. I then trained on GLSL, a graphic card language for interacting with shaders.

A training course that will leave a trace

.

The project in which I was involved did not have a UI/UX designer as such, so I got used to it. to postpone my research methods and the process I followed to achieve my goals. Thus, at the end of my internship, more than 160 pages of reports were delivered in addition to my missions.

Trainee during confinement

Because of the health crisis of 2020, I was forced to spend three and a half months teleworking. Far from this that I was looking for when I came to a large company, I was however able to see the strength of this one at provide resources to enable distance working under good conditions. Without having been Forgotten, the key word of this training course was for me autonomy and proactivity. This is how I a lot of training and I consolidated my rigour in my work, but I also had to be proactive. to cope with schedule changes due to containment.
